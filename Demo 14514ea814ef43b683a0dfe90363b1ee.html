Neptune Screen:

  • Zipping past Uranus, we finally arrive at Neptune, the outer most planet of our solar system.
  • We've traveled a total of 2.8 billion miles, or 30 au (astronomical units, 1 au is the distance from Earth to the sun).
  • It took one of the fastest thing humans ever made, the Voyager 2 probe, 12 years to get to this point. And it was traveling 20 times faster than a bullet. (19km/s)
  • Looking back.

Travelling Screen:

  • Picking up speed again, we now travel at 30 times the speed of light. At this speed, you can go around the earth (7.5*however many times speed of light) times a second.

Pale blue dot screen:

  • On Feb 14th, 1990, the Voyager 1 probe has well passed any planets and heading towards interstellar space. Under NASA's command, it turned around to take one last look at the planet Earth.
  • Came to be known as the Pale Blue Dot, the earth can be seen, less than a pixel in size, floating in a sun beam, ever so insignificant.

Interstellar travel:

  • We now fast travel for one last time, to visit our closest neighbor, the Alpha Centauri system. We'll have to travel about 6000 times the distance we covered so far, so sit tight.
  • Later in his book, astronomer Carl Sagan reflects on the significance of this photo.
  • "Look again at that dot. That's here. That's home. That's us. On it, everyone you love, everyone you know, everyone you ever heard of, every human being who ever was, lived out their lives."
  • Looking back on history, how arrogant it was to believe we're the center of the universe.
  • Astronomy has, timer after time, reminded us of our humble place in the grand scale of the universe.

Hubble Ultra Deep Field:

  • This is the Hubble Ultra Deep Field, a dark patch of sky covering roughly the area of a pin held at arm's length.
  • In this photo, 10,000 galaxies can be seen. Any one of them, contains as much as 100 billion stars.
  • We now believe there are also 100 billion galaxies out there. That's 10 sextillion stars! This is what it looks like written out: 10,000,000,000,000,000,000,000.
  • Too big to understand? Try this. For every grain of sand on earth, there is a star out there, as big as our sun. Scientists now believe half of those stars have habitable planets around them.
  • With that in mind, how incredibly arrogant it would be to think that Earth is the only planet where life exhists.
  • Pick a galaxy in this photo. Imagine there could be the most amazing things we have never even dreamed of. There could be civilizations of all proportions and there could be the most beautiful sights you have ever seen.

Distance Comparison:

  • And finally we arrive at our closest star, Proxima Centauri. 4.2 light years away. If the earth was 100 pixels wide, the distance we just traveled to scale would stretch 2 times around the Earth.
  • And this is how far that is compared to the our milky way galaxy.
  • Yep, we virtually didn't move at all. In fact, all the stars you can see in the night sky are only this far away.

Ending:

  • Space is huge, to say the least, but it doesn't mean our actions are meaningless. It means that we are to cherish our only home, and to also understand our humble position in the vastness of the universe. To quote Carl Sagan once again, "It underscores our responsibility to deal more kindly with one another, and to preserve and cherish the pale blue dot, the only home we've ever known."
